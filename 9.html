TESTING

 


      A variety of tests will be conducted during the early stages of development for the project, the first test will be on the functionality and features to make sure that the app will work properly and be able to support the features that it is stated to have before it can be released for public use. The way it will be tested is by gathering a group of people that vary in ages from as young as 15 to the age of 60, they will be asked test all the basic functionalities such as browsing restaurants, adding food to their cart, adding a payment method and placing an order through a regular delivery driver and robot. 


     The second test is on its durability, we want the robot to be able to withstand the most common accidents that the robot will typically face, which is colliding with vehicles and from people who may purposefully try to damage the robot and steal whatever is inside of it. To complete this we will have the robot collide with vehicles, pedestrians, drop test and most importantly waterproof testing to make sure that it can make delivers on rainy days. This is to understand the amount of damage the robot can take before it becomes immobilised. 


     The third test is to determine the maximum amount of load that the robot can handle while still moving at a steady speed of 5mph and the amount of food that we want the robot to be able to carry is around 10kg. To do this we will first put 10kg worth of food inside the robot and see if it can maintain 5mph, if it can’t we will continue to decrease the weight slightly till we achieve a steady speed of 5mph. Then determine what physical changes will need to be made to meet the requirements. 


     The fourth test is on security and sturdiness, to see whether it can prevent thieves from stealing the food, by having strong male’s volunteers attempt to break open the robot and steal whatever is inside it. The alarms and security cameras feature on the robot will also be tested to ensure that cameras are recording, alarms are loud and are going off when it is supposed to. 


     The fifth test is to test the accuracy and speed of the robot, to see if it can successfully deliver the food to several different addresses in different suburbs, whilst also being able to navigate itself around objects and people during the delivery trip. This test is also to ensure that the robot is moving at a speed of 4mph so that it won’t take too long for the customer to receive their food whilst also not endangering the safety of public. Overall, it minimises the chances of an accident occurring and allows the robot to be street legal. 


      We will know that we have succeeded in ours tests when the results from the tests mentioned above satisfy the criteria made for the robot.
