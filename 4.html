AIMS

 


 

MAIN PROJECT AIMS AND GOALS


     Our main project aim is to create an affordable food delivery app powered by an intelligent and efficient Artificial Intelligence. This is our main project aim because upon project completion we want to be a competitive force in the marketplace. Whereby cost cuttings during app development and robot infrastructure is passed down to consumers. The team endeavours for cost effective app and robot development, without sacrifice to fundamentals such as safety and IT security. 


     The following goals underlie our aim to facilitate project completion.


     Our first goal is to partner up with a competitive app development company, this will help reduce costs to consumers. Such as - http://mymobilefans.com/solutions/platform/ 


     Our second goal in our Food Delivery Project is to partner up with a successful AI company. We need smart robots to complete food deliveries, promptly and accurately. Such as - https://www.starship.xyz/business/ 


     Our third goal is for the app to be reliable and competitive to typical restaurant prices. We will achieve this by reducing technology costs such as choice of app platform and choice of Robot 


     Our fourth goal is robot speed, safety, reliability and accuracy. We will work closely with the AI company - https://www.starship.xyz/business/ Starship Technologies to find where out where we can improve safety and decrease hazards. In terms of speed the robot needs to deliver quickly but safely. Reliability relates to battery life, warranties and general wear and tear issues that would need to be addressed. Home and restaurant accuracy are another factor the team will be looking into, throughout the report. 


     Our fifth goal for this project is to implement 2 competitive advantages. The first feature we seek to implement is using phone Near-Field Communication (NFC), which can be used to unlock the food delivery robot to access food. The second special feature is fast wireless charging. The goal is to create a wireless charging pad for the robot to return to, similar to a charging base, which fits neatly into a restaurant.


 

IF THINGS DON'T GO AS EXPECTED / COMMON ISSUES


If there are issues concerning the food delivery app, the team will look towards working with the current app developer to resolve outstanding issues. Small app issues such as If large IT problems persist within the app such as lag, app crashes and security issues, the team may source another app developer. 


     In the event the AI company becomes unreliable, such as issues between the app and the robot, we will work with the chosen AI company to overcome the connection issues. When there and difficulties with the robot itself such as battery life or general wear and tear such as a broken lid or flat tyre. The team will investigate and determine if it can be claimed under warranty. If there are constant reliability issues, switching Robot companies can be considered under these circumstances. 


     The project mainly revolves around the app and robot delivery itself. Above are some common issues we can expect when things go wrong. In worst case scenarios such as huge app or robot IT issues, the project team may hold emergency meetings when this criterion is met in order to rectify such problems.




WHICH PARTS SHOULD HAVE PRIORITIES OVER OTHERS?


The Food Delivery App platform is just as important as the Robot Deliverer. However, the food delivery app is much more IT concentrated. The team needs to consider more complex functions such as push notifications, user loyalty (rewarding frequent customers), social sharing of food, shopping cart, app appearance, favouriting restaurants and of course GPS mapping. These parts of the project will need a priority over the robot. The robot is very important to the project as well, however, will require less IT concentration in terms of tracking, delivery and overall safety and reliability concerns that would need to be addressed. 


     Between the two project fundamentals; the app and the robot. The food delivery app will take priority over the delivery robot.




IF WE HAPPEN TO ONLY HAVE ENOUGH TIME OR RESOURCES

FOR ON OF THE GOALS...


It would once again be the app platform. If the project was low on time or capital, the executive decision would be to concentrate on creating a reliable and mainly useable app. The app takes precedence over the robot once again because if the app does not work well or at all, it would be difficult to finalise app orders. This means no money coming in as a result. No money means no further advancements or updates for the current app or robot. 


     When resources are low, the robot will not be as concentrated on because, although hypothetically incomplete, may have tracking and location issues or poor reliability such as battery life. Will still, hopefully get food delivered from A to B, however long it might be. 


     As long as the app works and there’s money coming through to fund future updates and upgrades, there’s a chance to improve the project tomorrow, should the team happen to run low on resources today.
